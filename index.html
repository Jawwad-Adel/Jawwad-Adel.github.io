About LacerMate

Our Focus

At LacerMate we believe in inclusivity. Struggle to bend your back to tie your shoes? No problem. We have designed our product to make shoe tying easy and effortless for those who suffer from back pain.

Adaptive Technology

Our design makes it easy to fit the product on most types of shoes. No need to buy special shoes. Simply add on the device on to your existing shoes.

Market

Our product is the first on the market that is fully retrofittable and completely rids the need for bending. Other similar products on the market usually lack one or both of these qualities, are either designed for sport or aesthetically unpopular.

Project LacerMate

Retrofittable Stick-on Design

At LacerMate, our focus is on the user. To make the product as user-friendly as possible it is designed to work with in any type of shoe owing to its simple stick-on technology. Velcro strips attached to our inflatable pouch make it possible to fit our device firmly under the tongue of your shoes. The pouch being in this location means no more fiddling around with laces - just simply slide into your shoes and pump up the pouch.

  • Date: January 2020

Project LacerMate

Hands-free Inflation and Deflation

Our product is designed so you don't have to bend your back. The valves and pressure pump have been designed to be fully compatible and the magnetic connection means they will attach automatically without having to use your hands. The pump can be operated electronically with the press of a button for inflating the pouch and the presta valve means deflating is made easy by simply lifting your foot to connect onto the pressure release piece that comes with our pump.

  • Date: January 2020

Project LacerMate

Custom-shaped Pouch

Our inflatable pouches are designed to go under the tongue of your shoes and can be designed to wrap around your feet or sit on top of your toes - fully customisable for maximum comfort. The flocked PVC material used for the exterior of the pouch means a comfortable and soft feel against your feet.

Additional Information

Building Tools For the Website

The tools used for the website were GIT and GITHUB and the text editor Atom. A Bootstrap theme was used as the basis of the website. GitHub was chosen because of the large online community and open source content that can be utilised for guidance. Moreover, GitHub hosts source code projects in a variety of different programming languages and keeps track of the various changes made to every iteration. Continuous integration automatically builds and tests the code it goes into GitHub, preventing bugs from developing. Continuous deployment means that a deployment pipeline automatically picks up the changes. It means it is possible to streamline the code implementation so we could focus on the product. GitHub also makes it easy when working in a group to keep track of various changes different group members have made.

Domain Setup

Step1 1: Decide on an available domain name that is descriptive of the website, easy to remember. You will have the choice to register the domain on several different top-level domains if available such as .com, .org, .biz, and .net.
Step 2: Visit a domain name registrar such as gandi.net, Domain.com and input the chosen domain name.
Step 3: Choose a company with which to register and host your domain and ensure that they are GDPR compliant. This is the company that is responsible for maintaining the website online. Examples include GoDaddy, Hover and BlueHost.
Step 4: Decide how long you wish to hold the domain name for and pay for the domain name through the company chosen.
Step 5: Purchase a web hosting account and launch a website under your domain.

Question a

What Makes a Good Website?

Clarity and conciseness are key elements of a good website, i.e. information must be presented in an uncluttered way. Several tools are used to achieve this. Typography can be made use of for visual appeal. Negative space prevents the page from being too cluttered. It is good practice to replace text wherever possible with multimedia materials and visuals to engage the viewer and convey ideas. The home page should allow clear navigation through the website’s contents. Fast-loading pages are also an essential feature of a good website, as these ensure the reader is not bored or frustrated by the speed at which the content appears. The website should have a strong and clear message making it evident what the product is and what it does.

Question b

What is accessibility and what considerations you should make for it?

A website should be made inclusive and as accessible as possible to people who are disabled. This includes provision for the blind and deaf. Assistive technologies should be used where possible, including screen readers that vocalise the text on each web page, and alt tags which show the content being hovered over by the cursor. Smart colour coding is also a useful option for the visually impaired or the colour blind.

Question c

What is GDPR and what considerations should made for privacy on your website?

GDPR aims to protect privacy and regulates what can be done with data collected. GDPR refers to laws which help protect the website owners from privacy and data breaches. It seeks to ensure responsibility and consent. In the context of GDPR, cookies must be considered as a form of a consent. It is necessary for everyone in a team to be GDPR aware, and an information audit should be carried out with the objective of ensuring GDPR compliancy.


Considerations for GDPR compliance:

1. Cookie notifications: This makes the user aware that in order to view the website they must consent for the website to store or retrieve any information on a computer, smartphone or tablet. The user must agree with the terms and conditions and privacy policies. Implied consent is no longer allowed (you must click to opt-in). Moreover, the option must be unbundled meaning that each individual box must only have a single statement of agreement. There must be an easy way for a user to opt out and withdraw consent.

2. Cookie policy: This should tell the users what data is being collected and for what purpose. It must also mention if any third-party members can also access their data and what their intentions are with it. This should either involve a link to the third-party policies or require the relevant information to be moved to the website itself.

3. Privacy policy: This expands on the cookie policy by stating how the data is collected and stored. The data stored must be accessible to the user and, hence, there should be a way of contacting the owner to consult on what data of theirs has been stored. Additionally, users must be able to request that their own stored data be deleted from the servers.

4. Lead Captures: There may be no pre-ticked boxes in forms and users must consent to receiving email advertisements.

5. Payments: It must be ensured that payment gateways for financial transactions are GDPR compliant and their privacy policy should be accessible from the website. In addition, if the website is required to store the data before passing it onto the gateways then it must be removed after a reasonable period of time as this is personal information.

6. Third party advertisement tracking software: This is where third-party marketing automation software solutions are used in the website. One must ensure that these parties are also GDPR approved and do not store and use data in ways which have not been consented to. Users must be able to access this information in the privacy policy.

7. If the product is traded internationally it will be necessary to determine the lead data protection supervisory authority.

Question d

What marketing steps would you take to drive people to look at your website?

A number of marketing strategies can be employed to drive traffic towards the website and make the product popular. It is first necessary to identify the audience we should be targeting. Leading figures in industry can be partnered with to trial the product and write a blog on it on our website. Bloggers and social media influencers can be reached out to with an already large audience base to promote the product and include a link to the website on their personal blogs/vlogs. Since our product is aimed at people with back problems, posters including a QR code directing to the website can be put up in places such as hospital boards. Search Engine Optimisation strategies can be used, such as content optimization and creating backlinks from other websites, to make our website appear higher up on Google and other searches. Social media should be used as well to market and promote the product, including establishing a Twitter and Facebook page.
