Tell Me More

We Are a Small Team of Entrepreneurs Pioneering the Industry’s First all Automatic Machine Learning Resistor Sorter.

The Product

Futility is a product which not only separates used resistors from foreign components, it also sorts them by value, dramatically reducing labour time and cost. Futility sorts and allows for the reuse of resistors which otherwise are thrown out, making Futility a cost, effort and time-saving tool. The current prototype is projected to cost £126.53, representing real value for money when one considers the savings it will be bring about in terms of labour or un-recycled materials.

Computer Vision

Indeed, the Futility design is unique and superior to alternative solutions due to Futility’s computer vision features. This gives Futility the unique advantage that it can identify any component and reject non-resistor components such as capacitors and loose wires.

Market

In Futility, we have built a product which fills a gap in the market and which we are sure will make radical improvements to a large number of customers in business and industry. Futility supplies the great existing demand from educational facilities and R&D companies, such as Imperial Colleague London EEE department, but we believe its market could be even bigger. Due to the beautiful simplicity of the design, Futility is a product that is ready for scalability.

Building Tools For the Website

The tools used for the website were GIT and GITHUB and the text editor Sublime Text. We also used Bootstrap themes to start the website. GitHub was chosen because of a large online community which means there is lots of open source content on the Internet which means a lot of guidance which can be used. Moreover, hosts source code projects in a variety of different programming languages, and keeps track of the various changes made to every iteration. Continuous integration automatically builds and tests the code it goes into GitHub, preventing bugs from being deployed. Continuous deployment means that a deployment pipeline automatically picks up the changes. It means it is possible to streamline the code deployment so we could focus on the product. GitHub also makes it easy when working in a group to keep track of various changes different group members have made. Other choices would have been BitBucket, GitLab and Microsoft Team Foundation Server.

Project Futility

LED and Camera Setup

The Pi camera is placed directly above a moving conveyor belt where it can detect the passing resistors. The LED creates a more suitable condition for detection. After detection, a signal is sent to the dynamic resistor containers to position correctly.

  • Date: February 2019

Project Futility

Dynamic Resistor Container

The containers which store the sorted resistors are capable of rotary motion using stepper motors. The signal sent by the detection phase instructs the stepper motor on the rotation it must perform for the resistor to drop to the correct wedge. The wedges are also detachable to allow for easier unloading of the sorted resistors.

  • Date: February 2019

Project Futility

Assembly

The final design involves the user manually loading resistors onto the conveyor belt which is powered by a stepper motor. These are then sorted into their allocated bins by the identification of the bands using computer vision. The resistors are dropped into one of twelve wedges which will sort for one order of magnitude of the E12 resistor series.

  • Date: February 2019

Question a

What Makes a Good Website?

A good website should present its information in a clear, concise and uncluttered way. Negative space prevents the page from being too cluttered. Typography is used to make the page both clear and visually appealing. The home page should provide clear navigation through the content of the website. Multimedia material and visuals should also be used to engage the viewer and make clear certain ideas, concepts and products without the need for too much text. Fast-loading pages are also an essential feature of a good website, as these ensure the reader is not bored or frustrated by the speed at which the content appears. The website should have a strong and clear message making it evident what the product is and what it does.

Question b

What is accessibility and what considerations you should make for it?

A website should be as accessible as possible to people who are disabled. This includes provision for the blind and deaf. Assistive technologies should be used where possible, including screen readers that vocalise the text on each web page, and alt tags which show the content being hovered over by the cursor. Smart colour coding is also a beneficial idea for the visually impaired or the colour blind.

Question c

What is GDPR and what considerations should made for privacy on your website?

GDPR refers to laws which help protect the website owners from privacy and data breaches. GDPR aims to protect privacy and regulates what can be done with data collected. It seeks to ensure responsibility an consent. In the context of GDPR, cookies must be considered as a form of a consent. It is necessary for everyone in the group to be GDPR aware, and an information audit should be carried out with the objective of ensuring GDPR compliancy.
To make the website GDPR compliant:
1. Cookie notifications: This is a tab that must make the user aware that in order to view the website they must consent. The user must agree with the terms and conditions and privacy policies. Implied consent is no longer allowed (you must click to opt-in). Furthermore, the option must be unbundled meaning that each individual box must only have a single statement of agreement. There also must be an easy was for a user to opt out and withdraw consent.
2. Cookie policy: This should tell the users what data is being collected and what the uses of it are. It should also say if any third party members can also access their data and what their intentions are with it. This should either involve a link to the third party policies or require the general information to be moved to the website itself.
3. Privacy policy: Expands on the cookie policy by stating how the data is collected and stored. The data stored must be accessible to the user therefore there should be a way of contacting the owner to consult on what data of theirs has been stored. In addition, the users must have the capability to request that their own stored data be deleted form the servers. 4. Lead Captures: no pre-ticked boxes in forms and users must consent to receiving email advertisements.
5. Payments: must ensure that payment gateways for financial transactions are also GDPR compliant and their privacy policy should be accessible form the website. In addition, if the website is required to store the data before passing it onto the gateways then it must be removed after a reasonable period as it is considered personal information.
6. Third party advertisement tracking software: This is where third-party marketing automation software solutions are used in the website. One must ensure that these parties are also GDPR approved and do not store and use data in ways which have not been consented to by the users. This must also be information that the users can access in the privacy policy.
7. In the event that the product will be traded internationally it will be necessary to determine the lead data protection supervisory authority.

Question d

What marketing steps would you take to drive people to look at your website?

A number of different marketing techniques can be used to raise the profile of our website and drive users towards it. Firstly, it will be necessary to identify the target audience for the product. Leading industry figures can be contacted with a view to them trialing the product and then perhaps guest blogging on the website to drive up traffic. Search Engine Optimisation strategies can be used, such as content optimization and creating backlinks from other websites, to make our website appear higher up on Google and other searches. Social media should be used as well to market and promote the product, including establishing a Twitter page for the team and a Facebook page. Designating a social media member of the team to regularly post and update about the product will enable us to establish and build up a large audience and direct people towards the website. We can also reach out to bloggers who already have large audiences to comment on our product and to promote us in their blog material.

Question e

The next steps you would take given more time for your product website? – how would you expand and what steps you would take i.e. e-commerce, etc?

If we had more time, we would develop the website from being merely an advertisement for the product to becoming a site where the product is sold. This would mean that it would be necessary to have the ability to sell the product on the website, for payment to be carried out online, and for GDPR compliance to be considered. With more time, accessibility would be improved so that disabled users could also gain full access to the website. Animations could also be developed to make the website more visually appealing and dynamic for the user. Videos of the product working could also be used to show the product’s capabilities and to give users a clearer idea of its functionality and appeal.
